# DOC-100 — EEOS Productivity & Work Operating Model (PWOM)

**Version:** 1.0

**Priority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Core Architecture)

**Status:** Foundation Document

---

# Purpose

The Productivity & Work Operating Model defines **how every user works inside EEOS**.

Every employee, student, parent, faculty, manager, CEO, finance executive, sales counsellor, editor, administrator, and vendor will experience EEOS through a unified personal workspace.

Modules do not define work.

**Work defines modules.**

---

# Vision

Traditional ERP systems force users to navigate between modules.

EEOS brings work to the user.

Instead of asking:

> "Which module should I open?"

Users should ask:

> **"What do I need to do next?"**

---

# EEOS Work Philosophy

```text
Organization creates Work

↓

Work creates Tasks

↓

↓

```

# EEOS Golden Productivity Rules

### Rule 1

Every user has exactly one workspace.

---

### Rule 2

Every activity becomes either

Task

Schedule

Inbox

Approval

Notification

---

### Rule 3

Nothing should require users to search through modules.

Work comes to the user.

---

### Rule 4

Every task must have

Owner

Assignee

Priority

Due Date

Status

---

### Rule 5

Every event must have

Location

Participants

Resources

Duration

Agenda

---

### Rule 6

Every notification must be actionable.

Notifications should open the exact record requiring attention.

---

### Rule 7

Every approval must be completed from Inbox.

No navigation required.

---

### Rule 8

Every user starts their day from **My Workspace**.

Modules are secondary.

# Final Principle

> **EEOS is designed to eliminate confusion. Every user should know exactly what to do, when to do it, where to do it, and how to do it—without switching between modules or searching for information. The Personal Workspace is the operating center of the Enterprise Operating System, making every individual connected, organized, accountable, and productive.**
